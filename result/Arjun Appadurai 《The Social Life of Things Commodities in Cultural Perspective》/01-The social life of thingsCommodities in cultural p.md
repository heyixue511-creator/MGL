### 一、核心主题 / Core Theme

This is the front matter (title page, copyright, table of contents) of an edited volume establishing a new anthropological approach to commodities. ### 四、核心论证 / Key Arguments

1. Things, like persons, have social lives -- they are not static entities with fixed value but move through different cultural contexts, regimes of value, and historical conditions that shape their meaning and status.
2. Commoditization is not a category of objects but a phase or process: objects can become commodities (be commoditized), exit commodity status (be de-commoditized or singularized), and re-enter it, depending on cultural and political circumstances.
3. The study of commodities requires an interdisciplinary, comparative methodology spanning anthropology, history, archaeology, and economics, grounded in specific ethnographic and historical cases across diverse world regions.
4. Value is politically constructed: what makes something a commodity, how it is exchanged, and what it is worth are outcomes of social negotiations, power relations, and cultural frameworks, not of intrinsic properties or universal market laws.
