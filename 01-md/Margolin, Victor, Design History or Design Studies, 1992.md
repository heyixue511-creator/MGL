                            And yet, despite these activities, there is little to show that
                              could gain recognitionfor design historyas a solid field of academic
                                 study. I do not wish to take away from the benefit it has had on

                           But I do want to address the issue of what has or has not been
                             accomplished to establish design history as a productive scholarly

                           Among the issues that had to be confrontedwhen the field
                            began was that of subjectmatter.It became importantto mark the
                                subject "design" with boundaries that would shape the develop-
                          ment of historicalaccounts.In the late 1970s,JohnBlake,an admin-
                                   istrator at the British Design Council, urged that design history
                          become "a kind of coagulationof ideas" that could develop into "a
                               recognizable body of knowledge which can be unequivocally
                                labeled 'design history'-not as an appendage of the history of art,
                              not as an appendage of the history of architecture, not as an
                            appendage of the history of technology or of anything else for that
                             matter-though with obvious connectionswith all these things."'
