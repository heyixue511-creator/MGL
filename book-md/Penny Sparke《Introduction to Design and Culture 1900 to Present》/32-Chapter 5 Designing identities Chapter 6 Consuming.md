## Chapter 5 Designing identities

## RECOMMENDED READING

A number of studies have focused on design as an ideological tool in the hands of nations. Paul Greenhalgh's Ephemeral Vistas: The Expositions Universelles, Great Exhibitions, and World's Fairs 1851—1939 (Manchester: Manchester University Press, 1988) provides an overview of all the important exhibitions in the first half of the twentieth century at which design made an appearance. Wendy Caplan's collection of essays, entitled Designing Modernity: The Arts of Reform and Persuasion 1885-1945 (Miami/London: Wolfsonian/Thames and Hudson, 1995) is a useful account of the ways in which a number of nations sought to exploit the links between design and modernity. In his hook Design in Germany 1870–1918 (London: Trcfoil, 1986), John Heskett explores Germany's strategic relationship with modern design while, in Designs on Modernity: Exhibiting the City in 1920s Paris (Manchester: Manchester University Press, 1998), Tag Gronberg shows how France developed a quite different model of modernity through design.
