## S: Meaning?

P: Meaning that it’s not just a transparent windowpane, transporting without deformation some information about your study. ‘There is no in-formation, only trans-formation.’ I assume that you agree with this ANT slogan? Well, then this is surely also true of your PhD thesis, no?

Depending on what happens in it, there is or there is not an actor and there is or there is not a network being traced. S: The problem is that’s not what my supervisor wants! He wants my case studies to ‘lead to some useful generalization’. He does not want ‘mere description’. P: You should panic only if your actors were not doing that constantly as well, actively, reflexively, obsessively. It’s not another layer that you would have to add to the ‘mere description’. Don’t try to shift from description to explanation: simply go on with the description. What your own ideas are about your company is of no interest whatsoever compared to how this bit of the company itself has managed to spread.

S: But if my people don’t act, if they don’t actively compare, standardize, organize, generalize, what do I do? I will be stuck! I won’t be able to add any other explanations.

S: You mean when there is no trace I should remain silent?

P: Incredible! Would you raise this question in any of the natural sciences? It would sound totally silly. It takes a social scientist to claim that they can go on explaining even in the absence of any information! Are you really prepared to make up data?

S: But what about invisible entities acting in some hidden ways?

P: Nothing. It would be great, but is it feasible? It means that, whatever a given concrete pendulum does, it will add no new information to the law of falling bodies. S: Isn’t that an ideal explanation?

P: That’s just the problem. It’s an ideal squared: the ideal of an ideal explanation. I doubt somewhat that your company’s subsidiary behaves that way. S: Minus the perturbations . . .
