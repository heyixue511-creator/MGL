# 'A novel

book which leads us to question the objects which make up our everyday surroundings. It provides an accessible and stimulating way to think about the gendering processes which contribute to our intimate and everyday lives'

Beverley Skeggs, Centre for Women's Studies, University of Lancaster

From Barbie and Action Man to guns via bicycles, perfume and trainers, The gendered object is an intriguing collection of new writing on the way in which objects of everyday life are made socially acceptable and 'appropriate' for women or men

What does the Strawberry Shortcake doll tell us about views of the adult female body? When does the necktie become antiestablishment? How does a woman relate to a washing-machine? And can a hearing aid really be gendered? These questions are answered and many others raised in this entertaining study of design for men and women.

This book will make fascinating reading for students and lecturers of design and art history, cultural studies and gender studies.
